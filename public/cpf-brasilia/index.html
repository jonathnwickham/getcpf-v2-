How to Get a CPF in Brasilia

Brasilia is the capital of Brazil and home to every foreign embassy in the country. If you are a diplomat, embassy worker, government contractor, or simply a foreigner passing through the capital, getting a CPF here has some unique advantages. The offices in Brasilia's Plano Piloto area are among the most experienced in Brazil when it comes to processing foreign applications.

Why Brasilia is different

Brasilia is not like other Brazilian cities. It was purpose-built in the 1960s as the federal capital, and its layout reflects that. The city is organized into numbered blocks and sectors rather than traditional neighborhoods with organic street patterns. This can be disorienting at first, but it also means government offices are concentrated in predictable areas.

The biggest advantage of getting your CPF in Brasilia is the staff. Because the city is full of embassies, international organizations, and diplomatic missions, the government offices here process foreign documents regularly. Staff at Plano Piloto locations have seen passports from every country and are familiar with the quirks of foreign names, unusual document formats, and non-standard address situations. This means fewer surprises and less chance of being turned away for something trivial.

Brasilia is also where the Receita Federal has its national headquarters. While you will not go to the headquarters building itself for a CPF application, the local CAC (Centro de Atendimento ao Contribuinte) offices in the capital tend to be well-staffed and efficiently run.

Best places to get a CPF in Brasilia

You have two options: Correios (post office) or Receita Federal. Both are viable in Brasilia, and both have reliable locations in the Plano Piloto area.

Correios (recommended for speed)

The Correios charges R$7 (about $1.50) and processes CPF applications on the spot. Not every branch offers this service. In Brasilia, these areas have reliable Correios branches for CPF:

  • ✓ Asa Sul - central location within Plano Piloto, well-connected by bus
  • ✓ Asa Norte - the other wing of Plano Piloto, equally accessible
  • ✓ Setor Comercial Sul - commercial district, busy but consistently available for CPF

Receita Federal (CAC Brasilia)

The Receita Federal CAC in Brasilia is a strong option. The service is free, and because this is the capital, the office is well-staffed. Staff are experienced with foreign applications. You may need to schedule through the Receita Federal website, and processing can take a few business days compared to same-day service at Correios. But if you prefer dealing directly with the tax authority, this is a solid choice.

Tips for getting your CPF in Brasilia

  • Plano Piloto offices are your best bet. The core of Brasilia, the Plano Piloto, is where government services are concentrated. Offices in Asa Sul and Asa Norte handle foreign applications regularly and staff know what to expect.
  • Go early. Correios opens at 9am. Mornings are quieter. Brasilia is a government city, and offices can get busy around lunchtime as civil servants run errands.
  • Satellite cities are less reliable. Brasilia's satellite cities like Taguatinga, Ceilandia, and Samambaia have Correios branches, but they are less likely to offer CPF services for foreigners. Stick to Plano Piloto.
  • Bring a pen. You will fill out a paper form in Portuguese. Having your own pen speeds things up.
  • Staff may speak some English. Brasilia has more English-speaking government workers than most Brazilian cities, thanks to the diplomatic community. You may get lucky, but do not count on it. A Portuguese cheat sheet is still a good idea.
  • Distances are deceptive. Brasilia is spread out and designed for cars. What looks close on a map can be a 20-minute drive. Plan your route and consider using a ride-share app.

Understanding Brasilia's layout

Brasilia's Plano Piloto is shaped like an airplane when seen from above. The two "wings" are Asa Sul (south wing) and Asa Norte (north wing). Most residential blocks, shops, and services are along these wings. The "fuselage" running through the middle is the Eixo Monumental, where you will find government buildings, the bus terminal, and major landmarks.

Addresses in Brasilia use a block-and-sector system. For example, SQS 308 means "Superquadra Sul 308." It is logical once you understand it, but confusing at first. When heading to a Correios for your CPF, use a maps app rather than trying to navigate by address alone.

What to expect at the office

Walk in and tell the attendant you need a CPF. They will give you a form to fill out in Portuguese. You write your name, nationality, date of birth, passport number, and Brazilian address. Hand over the form with your passport and address proof. Pay R$7 at Correios. In most cases, you receive your CPF number printed on a receipt right there. The whole process takes about 15 minutes if you have everything ready.

Frequently asked questions

Can I go to the Receita Federal headquarters for my CPF?

The Receita Federal headquarters is in Brasilia, but individual CPF applications are handled at local CAC offices, not the headquarters building. The Plano Piloto CAC is the most common option for foreigners.

Is it easier to get a CPF in Brasilia than other cities?

Brasilia offices tend to be well-organized and staff are experienced with foreign applications due to the large diplomatic community. Wait times can be shorter than in Sao Paulo.

Do I need an appointment?

No appointment needed at Correios. At Receita Federal CAC offices, you may need to check their website for scheduling. Correios is always walk-in.

Can I get a CPF in Brasilia on a tourist visa?

Yes. You do not need a work visa, diplomatic visa, or residency. Foreigners on any visa type can apply at Correios or Receita Federal in Brasilia.
