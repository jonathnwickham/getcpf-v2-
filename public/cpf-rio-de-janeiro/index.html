How to Get a CPF in Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro is one of the most popular destinations for foreigners in Brazil. Whether you are here for the beaches, the culture, or a longer stay, getting a CPF should be high on your list. Here is where to go, what to bring, and how to avoid common mistakes in Rio.

Best places to get a CPF in Rio

Correios (recommended)

Correios is the fastest option. R$7 fee, walk-in, often same-day. Not every branch processes CPF applications, so go to the right one:

  • ✓ Copacabana - high tourist area, staff experienced with foreigners
  • ✓ Centro - main post office, busy but always available
  • ✓ Botafogo - popular expat neighborhood, less crowded
  • ✓ Leblon/Ipanema - upscale area, smaller branches, shorter waits
  • ✓ Barra da Tijuca - if you are staying in the west zone

Receita Federal

The Receita Federal office in Centro also processes CPF applications for free. Wait times are longer and some scheduling may be required. For most foreigners, Correios is the better choice.

What to bring

  • ✓ Passport (original, valid)
  • ✓ Proof of address (utility bill, rental contract, or host declaration letter)
  • ✓ R$7 in cash for Correios
  • ✓ Passport photocopy (some offices ask, bring one just in case)

Tips for Rio specifically

  • Go early, especially in summer. Rio's Correios offices get busy after 10am. During Carnival season and holidays, expect longer waits.
  • Copacabana is your best bet as a tourist. Staff there handle foreign CPF applications regularly and know the process well.
  • Avoid Mondays and Fridays. These tend to be the busiest days. Tuesday through Thursday is ideal.
  • Bring water. If you end up waiting, Rio's heat makes standing in line uncomfortable. Small branches usually have air conditioning.
  • Keep your passport safe. You need to bring the original. Do not leave it at the hotel. Consider taking an Uber directly to the office rather than walking with it.

Address proof in Rio

If you are in a hotel in Copacabana or an Airbnb in Ipanema, you probably do not have a Brazilian utility bill. A host declaration letter is your best option. GET CPF generates this in the correct format, including your host's details and a statement confirming your address.

Frequently asked questions

Which Correios in Rio does CPF for foreigners?

Branches in Copacabana, Centro, Botafogo, and Leblon are reliable. GET CPF identifies the best one for your location.

Is the process different in Rio?

No. Same documents, same fee, same procedure as anywhere in Brazil.

Can I get a CPF near Copacabana?

Yes. Multiple Correios branches in the area process CPF applications. Staff are used to foreigners.

Do I need an appointment?

No. Correios is walk-in. Go early for the shortest wait.
