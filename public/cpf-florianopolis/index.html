How to Get a CPF in Florianopolis

Florianopolis is one of the most popular destinations in Brazil for digital nomads, surfers, and remote workers. The island draws thousands of foreigners every year with its beaches, coworking spaces, and laid-back lifestyle. If you are staying more than a few days, getting a CPF should be near the top of your list. Here is how to do it without wasting a trip to the wrong office.

Why Florianopolis is different

Florianopolis is an island city. Most of the popular neighborhoods where foreigners stay, like Lagoa da Conceicao, Campeche, and Jurere, are spread across the island's coastline. That sounds great for surfing, but it creates a practical problem: not every neighborhood has a Correios branch that processes CPF applications. Some beach areas have small post offices that handle mail and packages but do not offer CPF registration services.

This catches a lot of foreigners off guard. You might be staying in Campeche, walk to the nearest Correios, and discover they cannot help you. Then you have to take a bus or ride to Centro, which can take 30 to 45 minutes depending on traffic and where you are on the island.

Best places to get a CPF in Florianopolis

You have two options: Correios (post office) or Receita Federal. For most foreigners, Correios is faster and simpler.

Correios (recommended)

The Correios charges R$7 (about $1.50) and processes CPF applications on the spot. In Florianopolis, the most reliable branch for CPF services is in Centro, on the mainland-connected side of the island. This is the main post office and consistently handles foreign CPF applications.

  • ✓ Centro - main Correios branch, most reliable for CPF services, staff experienced with foreigners
  • ✓ Trindade - near UFSC university, worth checking availability before visiting
  • ✓ Continente (mainland) - if you are staying on the mainland side, branches here may also offer CPF services

Branches to avoid for CPF

Smaller branches in beach neighborhoods like Lagoa da Conceicao, Campeche, Jurere, and Barra da Lagoa are unlikely to process CPF applications. These are satellite offices that handle basic postal services. Do not assume they can help with CPF registration. Always confirm before making the trip.

Receita Federal

Florianopolis has a Receita Federal office in Centro. The service is free but may require scheduling through their website. Wait times vary and the process can take a few business days compared to the same-day service at Correios.

Tips for getting your CPF in Florianopolis

  • Go to Centro first. Do not gamble on a beach neighborhood branch. The Centro Correios is the safest bet for CPF services in Florianopolis.
  • Go early in the morning. Correios opens at 9am. The first hour tends to be quiet. Floripa moves at a slower pace than Sao Paulo, but lines still build up by midday.
  • Plan for travel time. If you are staying in Lagoa da Conceicao or Campeche, budget 30 to 45 minutes to get to Centro. Bus routes run frequently but the island's roads get congested during peak hours.
  • Floripa offices are less busy than big cities. One advantage of getting your CPF here instead of Sao Paulo or Rio is that wait times are generally shorter. You may be in and out in 10 minutes.
  • Staff may not speak English. Even in a tourist-friendly city like Florianopolis, Correios staff rarely speak English. A Portuguese cheat sheet with key phrases will help you through the process.
  • Bring a pen. You will fill out a paper form entirely in Portuguese. Having your own pen saves time.

Neighborhoods where foreigners stay

Understanding the geography helps you plan your CPF trip. Here is where most foreigners end up in Florianopolis:

  • Lagoa da Conceicao - the hub for digital nomads. Coworking spaces, restaurants, and nightlife. About 25 minutes from Centro by car.
  • Campeche - popular with surfers and longer-term visitors. Quieter, more residential. About 30 minutes from Centro.
  • Jurere - upscale beach area on the north of the island. Beautiful but far from Centro, roughly 40 minutes.
  • Centro - the urban core. Not where most foreigners choose to stay, but where you will go for CPF services. Close to the bus terminal and well-connected.

No matter which neighborhood you are based in, plan a single trip to Centro to get your CPF sorted. Combine it with other errands if you can, since getting across the island takes time.

What to expect at the office

Walk in and tell the attendant you need a CPF. They will give you a form to fill out in Portuguese. You write your name, nationality, date of birth, passport number, and Brazilian address. Hand over the form with your passport and address proof. Pay R$7. In most cases, you receive your CPF number printed on a receipt right there. The whole process takes about 15 minutes if you have everything ready.

Frequently asked questions

Can I get a CPF in Florianopolis on a tourist visa?

Yes. You do not need a work visa or residency to get a CPF. Foreigners on any visa type, including tourist visas, can apply at a Correios or Receita Federal office in Florianopolis.

Which Correios in Florianopolis does CPF for foreigners?

Not every branch offers CPF services. The Centro branch is the most reliable option. Some beach neighborhood branches do not process CPF applications at all. GET CPF identifies the correct office for your location.

Is it easier to get a CPF in Florianopolis than in Sao Paulo?

Generally yes. Florianopolis is smaller and less crowded, so wait times tend to be shorter. The trade-off is fewer branches offer CPF services, so you need to go to the right one.

Do I need a local address in Florianopolis to get a CPF?

You need proof of a Brazilian address. If you are in an Airbnb, hostel, or staying with a friend, a host declaration letter works. GET CPF generates this letter for you.

How long does it take to get a CPF in Florianopolis?

About 15 minutes. At most Correios locations in Florianopolis, you get your CPF number the same day, often within minutes.
