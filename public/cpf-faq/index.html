CPF basics

What is a CPF number?▼

CPF stands for Cadastro de Pessoas Fisicas (Registry of Individual Persons). It is an 11-digit tax identification number issued by Brazil's Receita Federal. Every Brazilian citizen has one, and foreigners living in or visiting Brazil can apply for one too.

Do foreigners need a CPF in Brazil?▼

Yes. You need a CPF to open a bank account, get a phone plan, use Pix, rent an apartment, order food delivery, shop online, sign up for a gym, and access most digital services in Brazil. Without one, you are locked out of daily life.

Is a CPF like a social security number?▼

Not exactly. Unlike a US SSN, a CPF is not considered sensitive or secret. Brazilians share their CPF constantly for purchases, deliveries, and sign-ups. It is more like a tax ID that is woven into daily life.

What is the difference between CPF and CNPJ?▼

CPF is for individuals. CNPJ is for businesses (Cadastro Nacional de Pessoas Juridicas). As a foreigner, you need a CPF. You only need a CNPJ if you are opening a business in Brazil.

What is the difference between CPF and RNE/CRNM?▼

CPF is a tax ID available to anyone, including tourists. RNE (now called CRNM) is a foreigner registration card for those with visas or residency. They are separate documents. You can have a CPF without an RNE.

Does a CPF expire?▼

No. Once issued, your CPF number is yours for life. However, non-residents living abroad may need to update their registration periodically through the Receita Federal website or app.

Can I have more than one CPF?▼

No. Each person can only have one CPF number. If you applied before and already have one, the system will flag it. Check the Receita Federal website before applying again.

Documents and requirements

What documents do I need to get a CPF?▼

Foreigners need a valid passport to apply for a CPF. You need your original passport, a photocopy of the photo page, and proof of a Brazilian address (utility bill, rental contract, or host declaration letter).

Can I use my national ID card instead of a passport?▼

No. Foreigners need a valid passport to apply for a CPF. National ID cards from other countries are generally not accepted. Always bring your passport.

Do I need to bring photocopies of my passport?▼

Yes. Most Receita Federal offices require a photocopy of your passport's photo page. Some offices have a copy machine, but do not count on it. Bring copies prepared in advance.

What counts as proof of address?▼

A utility bill (water, electricity, internet) in your name, a rental contract, or a host declaration letter from whoever you are staying with. Hotel booking confirmations are sometimes accepted but not guaranteed.

What is a host declaration letter?▼

A declaracao de hospedagem is a signed letter from a Brazilian resident confirming you are staying at their address. It must include their full name, CPF number, address, and signature. Some offices also require a copy of their ID.

Can I use someone else's address?▼

Yes. If you are staying with a friend, Airbnb host, or anyone with a Brazilian address, they can provide a host declaration letter. This is the most common method for tourists and new arrivals.

What is the CPF application form?▼

A short form with your personal information that you submit at the Receita Federal office. Some offices have you fill it on-site, but arriving with a pre-printed copy speeds things up. Services like GET CPF generate this form for you automatically.

The application process

Where do I apply for a CPF?▼

At a Receita Federal CAC office (free) or at a Correios post office (R$7 fee). Major cities have multiple options. You can also apply at Brazilian consulates abroad or by email to regional Receita Federal offices.

What is a CAC office?▼

CAC stands for Centro de Atendimento ao Contribuinte (Taxpayer Service Center). These are the Receita Federal offices where you go in person for CPF registration and other tax services.

Do I need an appointment?▼

Most offices accept walk-ins for CPF registration. The online appointment system requires a CPF to book, which creates a catch-22 for first-time applicants. Walk-in is usually the only option for foreigners getting their first CPF.

What is the best time to go?▼

Arrive when the office opens (usually 7:00-8:00 AM). Tuesday through Thursday tends to be less busy. Avoid Mondays, the first week of the month, and afternoons. Some offices stop distributing queue numbers 30-60 minutes before closing.

Can I apply for a CPF by email?▼

Yes. Many Receita Federal regional offices accept CPF applications via email. You send scanned copies of your passport and proof of address. Processing takes 3-7 business days.

Can I get a CPF before arriving in Brazil?▼

Yes. Brazilian consulates abroad can process CPF applications through the e-consular system (econsular.itamaraty.gov.br). Availability varies by country. Most foreigners find it easier to apply in person once in Brazil.

Do I need to speak Portuguese?▼

No, but it helps. Most Receita Federal staff speak limited English. Having your documents prepared in advance and knowing a few key phrases makes the process smoother. Services like GET CPF provide all documents pre-filled so you just hand them over.

Do I get a physical CPF card?▼

No. Brazil stopped issuing physical CPF cards. You receive a printed receipt with your 11-digit CPF number. You can look up your number anytime on the Receita Federal website or app.

Timeline and costs

How long does it take to get a CPF?▼

At most Receita Federal offices, you receive your CPF number the same day, usually within 15-30 minutes. Some offices may take a few business days. Correios locations typically process it same-day.

How much does a CPF cost?▼

CPF registration at a Receita Federal office is completely free. At Correios post offices, there is a small fee of R$7 (about $1.30 USD). Services that help you prepare documents charge separately for the preparation work.

Can I get a CPF on a tourist visa?▼

Yes. You do not need a work visa or permanent residency. Foreigners on any visa type, including tourist visas, can apply for and receive a CPF.

Can I get a CPF if I am only staying 2 weeks?▼

Yes. There is no minimum stay requirement. Even tourists staying a few days can apply. Apply early in your trip so you can use it during your stay.

What you can do with a CPF

Can I open a Nubank account without a CPF?▼

No. Nubank, Inter, C6 Bank, and all Brazilian banks require a CPF to open an account.

Can I use Pix without a CPF?▼

No. Pix requires a bank account, which requires a CPF. Without Pix, you are limited to cash and international credit cards.

Do I need a CPF for iFood?▼

Yes. iFood, Brazil's largest food delivery app, requires a CPF to create an account.

Do I need a CPF for a phone plan?▼

Prepaid SIMs can sometimes be purchased without a CPF, but postpaid plans always require one. Even prepaid activation is increasingly requiring CPF registration.

Do I need a CPF to buy property in Brazil?▼

Yes. Foreigners can buy property in Brazil, but the purchase requires a CPF for the transaction and registration with the cartorio (notary office).

Can I invest in Brazil without a CPF?▼

No. Opening a brokerage account, investing in stocks on B3, or buying property all require a CPF.

Common problems

What if Receita Federal says they cannot process my CPF?▼

This sometimes happens at smaller branches. Your legal right to a CPF is established under Brazilian law. Try a larger Receita Federal office or the nearest Correios.

What if I am turned away because I am a foreigner?▼

Any foreigner can register for a CPF. This is your legal right. Try another Receita Federal office or the nearest Correios. Major capital city offices handle foreign applications most smoothly.

My CPF is suspended. What do I do?▼

A suspended CPF usually means you missed a required filing or update. You can regularize it online through the Receita Federal website or by visiting any office. The process is free.

I lost my CPF number. How do I find it?▼

Look it up on the Receita Federal website using your name and date of birth. You can also call their helpline or visit any office with your passport.

My name was formatted wrong on my CPF. Can I fix it?▼

Yes. You can update your name at any Receita Federal office. Bring your passport and supporting documents. The update is free and usually same-day.

Special situations

Can digital nomads get a CPF?▼

Yes. Digital nomads on tourist visas or the digital nomad visa (Brazil's digital nomad visa) can get a CPF. The process is the same as for any other foreigner.

Do children need a CPF?▼

Foreign children living in Brazil may need one for school, health insurance, or other services. Parents can apply on behalf of their children at a Receita Federal office.

Can I get a CPF if I have a criminal record?▼

Yes. A CPF is a tax identification number, not a background check. Criminal history does not affect your eligibility.

Can I get a CPF at the airport?▼

Some airports have Receita Federal offices, but they typically handle customs, not CPF applications. Do not count on getting a CPF at the airport.

Is the CPF process different in different states?▼

The process is the same nationwide, but individual offices may have slightly different hours and queue systems. Major capital city offices tend to handle foreigner applications most smoothly.

What changed in 2026

What is new about CPF rules in 2026?▼

Foreigners need a valid passport to apply for a CPF. National ID cards from Mercosur countries are no longer accepted. Non-residents abroad may also need to update their registration periodically.

What is the annual re-registration requirement?▼

Foreigners living abroad who hold a CPF may need to update their registration periodically through the Receita Federal website or app. If you miss it, your CPF may be suspended until you update.
