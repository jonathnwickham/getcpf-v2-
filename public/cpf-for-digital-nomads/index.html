CPF for Digital Nomads in Brazil: Complete Guide

Brazil is one of the best places in the world to work remotely. Affordable cost of living, reliable internet in major cities, great food, and a welcoming culture. But within your first few days, you will hit a wall: almost everything requires a CPF. Here is how to get one as a digital nomad.

Why digital nomads need a CPF

A CPF (Cadastro de Pessoas Fisicas) is Brazil's tax identification number. It is required for almost everything you need as a digital nomad:

  • ✓ Bank account with Nubank, Inter, or any Brazilian bank
  • ✓ Pix, the instant payment system that replaced cash and cards
  • ✓ Local SIM card from Vivo, Claro, or TIM (prepaid requires CPF too)
  • ✓ Apartment rental on QuintoAndar or through any landlord
  • ✓ Coworking memberships at WeWork, Selina, or local spaces
  • ✓ Food delivery on iFood and Rappi
  • ✓ Uber, 99, gym memberships, and subscription services

Without a CPF, you are stuck using cash, eating at restaurants, and relying on your home country's banking app. That gets old fast.

You can get a CPF on a tourist visa

This surprises most people. You do not need a work visa, residency, or the digital nomad visa (VITEM XIV) to get a CPF. A standard 90-day tourist visa is enough. Walk into a Correios (post office), bring your passport and address proof, pay R$7, and you will have your CPF number, often the same day.

The address proof problem

This is where most digital nomads get stuck. The CPF application requires proof of a Brazilian address. If you have a rental contract or utility bill, you are fine. But if you are staying in an Airbnb, hostel, or with a friend, you do not have those.

The solution is a host declaration letter (carta de declaracao). This is a signed letter from whoever you are staying with, stating that you reside at their address. It needs their name, CPF number, address, and signature. Some offices also ask for a copy of the host's ID.

Brazil's digital nomad visa

Brazil introduced the VITEM XIV digital nomad visa, which allows remote workers to stay for up to one year. It requires proof of income (at least $1,500/month) and health insurance. Even with this visa, you still need a CPF for daily life. The visa makes you legal for the stay. The CPF makes you functional.

Best cities for digital nomads (and where to get your CPF)

  • Sao Paulo - the business hub. Best coworking scene, fastest internet, most Correios options.
  • Florianopolis - beach and tech. Popular with nomads. Multiple Correios locations.
  • Rio de Janeiro - the classic. Great lifestyle, solid infrastructure.
  • Curitiba - underrated. Organized, affordable, European-influenced.
  • Recife/Olinda - emerging nomad hub in the northeast. Lower cost of living.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a CPF on a tourist visa?

Yes. A CPF is not tied to your visa type. You can apply on a standard 90-day tourist visa. It is required for bank accounts, phone plans, apartment rentals, and most apps.

Can I open Nubank without a CPF?

No. Nubank, Inter, and every Brazilian bank requires a CPF. You cannot use Pix or receive payments without one.

What about address proof in an Airbnb?

Use a host declaration letter from your Airbnb host or whoever you are staying with. GET CPF generates this for you in the correct format.

How long is a CPF valid?

Forever. Once issued, your CPF number is yours for life. It does not expire regardless of your visa status.

Can I get a CPF before arriving?

Some Brazilian consulates process CPF applications. Availability varies by country. Most nomads find it faster to apply in person at a Correios once they land.
